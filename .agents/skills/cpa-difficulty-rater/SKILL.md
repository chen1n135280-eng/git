# CPA Difficulty Rater

Rate inherent content complexity, not the learner's current mastery.

## Scale

- 1 star: definition, direct fact, or simple identification.
- 2 stars: one rule, one condition, or direct calculation.
- 3 stars: multiple steps, a journal entry, or a common exception.
- 4 stars: several interacting conditions, comprehensive judgment, or frequent confusion.
- 5 stars: cross-topic application, compound instruments, complex measurement, or material professional judgment.

## Rules

1. Base the rating on reasoning steps, interacting conditions, prerequisite load, exceptions, and examination confusion.
2. Do not increase difficulty merely because wording is long.
3. Provide one concise reason tied to the scale.
4. Require human adjustment support; never treat AI ratings as final.
5. Require plain-language explanation, teaching case, and mistakes for every point rated 3 stars or higher.
